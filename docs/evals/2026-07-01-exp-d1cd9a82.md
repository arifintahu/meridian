## 2. Diagnosis

The prior tune is **validated**: the run flipped from −$36.15 to +$10.75, stop-losses
dropped 8→2, and the worst loss was capped at −10.56% (was −22.24%). The strategy is
fee-positive — fees (+$36.40) outrun price/IL drag (−$25.65) — with profit concentrated
in ~11 long-held take-profit/trailing closes; the remaining 80 positions churn near
break-even as price leaves the single-sided below-range (67% OOR/pumped exits). No
screening signal separates winners from losers across either run, so thresholds stay
untouched. The one residual: the in-range drawdown rule still fired **0×** — both
stop-losses rode straight to −10 without the −8/15m soft cull engaging, and Chameleon
(low-vol 1.78, 390-min, reff 100) clearly dwelt in the −8…−10 band long enough to fire.
That is now low-impact (the stop caps the tail) but points at a possible drawdown-clock
engagement issue worth a separate code look — not a config lever.

## 5. Follow-up — tick-level root cause of the 0× drawdown firing (2026-07-01 16:03 SEAST)

Investigated the "watch item" above against the per-tick `position_snapshots` table
(PnL trajectory at ~10-min resolution; the clock itself advances every 20s via the
poller). **Conclusion: the rule is correct and correctly never-triggered — this is not
a bug, and not a clock-reset bug.** The three suspected causes all fall:

1. **Exit precedence** (`state.js:570` STOP_LOSS returns before the drawdown block at
   `:580`) — real but irrelevant. When a position genuinely sits above the stop (e.g.
   −9%) for the wait window, the rule *does* fire (18/18 unit tests in
   `test/in-range-drawdown.test.js` pass). Precedence only bites if a tick is
   simultaneously `≤ −10` and already has 15-min dwell — which never occurs.
2. **Clock reset on bounces** — not the cause. The grace-tolerant clock
   (`nextDrawdownClockState`, commit `2331f63`, 2026-06-24 13:29 UTC) was live before
   *both* runs started. The clock never resets because it never starts — PnL never
   enters the band.
3. **The −9.6% "stop loss" label** — correct. Chameleon's stored `close_reason` is
   `"Stop loss: PnL -10.41% <= -10%"`; the stop fired at −10.41% and the position
   recovered to −9.6% by the close-execution snapshot. Not a mislabel.

**The actual mechanism (tick evidence):** across all 91 positions, **zero were ever
sampled in the `(−10, −8]` band while in range.** The two stop-losses crossed the
entire 2%-wide band inside a single sub-10-min leg:

| Position | Trajectory (snapshot ts) | Band-crossing |
|---|---|---|
| Potato-SOL | −1.80% (t+50m) → **−10.56%** (t+57m) | ~7 min |
| Chameleon-SOL | healthy/positive for 371 min → +1.10% (t+371m) → −5.48% (t+381m) → **−10.41%** (t+391m) | ~10 min |

Chameleon — the supposed "390-min bleed" — was a 371-minute healthy hold (peaked +2%,
earned $2.34 fees) followed by a terminal gap-down. It never bled through the band. The
prior premise ("a 390-min in-range bleed must have dwelt in the band") was wrong about
the trajectory. The bound holds even at 20s poller resolution: the healthy→below-stop
transition completes within one ≤10-min gap, so no position can accrue the required
**15-min** in-band dwell before the −10 stop fires. The band width (2%) and wait (15m)
are jointly mismatched to the *loss shape* — single-sided memecoin losers don't
slow-bleed at −9%, they hold up then gap through −8→−10 in minutes.

**Fix — none (config or code).** The `−10` stop already handles the actual loss shape
and the run is profitable (+$10.75). Making the rule fire would mean a shallower stop
(≈−5%) or a velocity trigger applied to *every* position — but the tick data shows many
positions dip to −5%…−1% and recover to profit (Chameleon dipped repeatedly, recovered
to +2%), so a shallower trigger converts recoverable positions into realized losses and
adds churn to save ~$5 on two stops. Negative EV; declined. The `inRangeDrawdownExit`
stays **enabled and unchanged** — it costs nothing when idle and remains a safety net if
a genuine slow-bleed regime ever appears. Correcting the *mental model* (this rule is
working, not buggy) is the only change; earlier-cut exits are a strategy question for a
dedicated experiment, not a bugfix.
